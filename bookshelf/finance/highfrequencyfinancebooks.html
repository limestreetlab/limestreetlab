  • High Frequency Trading
  • Irene Aldridge
  • This book pretends to be comprehensive and authoritative but is actually superficial. It is a shallow summary of different academic studies (particularly Michel Dacorogna) and name-drops them in a line or a paragraph in each chapter as if the author understands all. I wonder who the intended audience is; a faint-of-heart can't understand, a practitioner can't find applications, a researcher can read the original research, a developer can't find relevant technology topics. Having said that, I still find it better than Ernie Chan's Quantitative Trading which is a total trash and will give it another glance (on backtesting and system setup as well as the few new talks in the 2nd edition) when I begin developing my system.

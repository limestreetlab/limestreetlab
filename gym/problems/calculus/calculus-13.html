Check whether the series $\sum_{n=2}^\infty \frac{1}{n(\ln{(n)})^3}$ converges.
Let's use the integral test and let $f(x) = \frac{1}{x\ln^3{(x)}}$, which is a decreasing function.

Let $u = \ln(x) \implies du = \frac{dx}{x}$, \begin{align} \int_2^\infty \frac{1}{x(\ln{(x)})^3}\,dx & = \int_{\ln{(2)}}^\infty \frac{1}{u^3}\,du\\ & = \left. -\frac{1}{2}u^{-2} \right|_{\ln{(2)}}^{\infty} \\ & = \frac{1}{2(\ln{2})^2} \end{align} As the improper integral has a limit, the series converges.

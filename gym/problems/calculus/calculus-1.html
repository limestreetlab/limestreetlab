Find $\int \arcsin(x) \,dx $.
Use integration by parts, $\int u \, dv = uv - \int v \, du$.

Let $ u = \arcsin(x)$ s.t. $ \frac{du}{dx} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} \implies du = \frac{dx}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}$ , and $ dv = dx $ s.t. $ v = x$,
then, $$ \int \arcsin(x) \,dx = x\arcsin(x) - \int \frac{x}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}}\, dx $$ For the second integral, let $ t = 1 - x^2 \implies \frac{dt}{dx} = -2x \implies dt = -2x\, dx$,
then, \begin{align} \int \frac{x}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}}\, dx & = \int \frac{-2}{-2} \frac{x}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}} \, dx \\ & = \int -\frac{1}{2} \frac{1}{\sqrt{t}} \, dt \\ &= -\frac{1}{2} \cdot 2t^\frac{1}{2} \\ &= -t^\frac{1}{2} = -\sqrt{1 - x^2} \end{align} Giving, $$ \int \arcsin(x) \,dx = x\arcsin(x) - -\sqrt{1 - x^2} = x\arcsin(x) + \sqrt{1 - x^2} $$

Find the radius of convergence for series $\sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{x^n}{n^2}$.
Recall we can find the radius of convergence $R$ of a power series $\sum a_n x^n$ using either $ L = \lim_{n\to\infty}|\frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n}|$ or $L = \lim_{n\to\infty}\sqrt[n]{|a_n|}$, and let $R = \frac{1}{L}$.

\begin{align} \left|\frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n}\right| & = \frac{1/(n+1)^2}{1/n^2} \\ & = \frac{1}{(n+1)^2}\frac{n^2}{1} \\ & = \left(\frac{n}{n+1}\right)^2 \\ & = \left(\frac{1}{1+\frac{1}{n}}\right)^2 \\ & = \frac{1}{(1+\frac{1}{n})^2} \\ & \implies \lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{1}{(1+\frac{1}{n})^2} = 1 \end{align} Hence, $R = \frac{1}{L} = \frac{1}{1} = 1$.

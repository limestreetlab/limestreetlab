Find $ \int x^2\sin(x)\,dx $.
Use integration by parts, namely $\int u\,dv = uv - \int v\,du$.

Let $ u = x^2 \implies du = 2x\,dx $, and $ dv = \sin(x)\,dx \implies v = -\cos(x) $, giving
$$ \int x^2\sin(x)\,dx = -x^2\cos(x) + 2\int x\cos(x)\,dx $$ Now let $ t = x \implies dt = dx$, and $ dz = \cos(x)\,dx \implies x = \sin(x)$, $$ \int x\cos(x)\,dx = x\sin(x) - \int\sin(x)\,dx = x\sin(x) + \cos(x) $$ Hence, $$ \int x^2\sin(x)\,dx = -x^2\cos(x) + 2x\sin(x) + 2\cos(x) $$

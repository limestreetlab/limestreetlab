Find $\int e^x \sin x \, dx $.
Use integration by parts, $\int u \, dv = uv - \int v \, du$.

Let $ u = e^x \implies \frac{du}{dx} = e^x$ and $du = e^x\, dx$, $dv = \sin x \,dx \implies v = -\cos x $,
then, $$ \int e^x \sin x \,dx = -e^x\cos x + \int e^x \cos x \,dx $$
For the second integral, let $t = e^x \implies dt = e^x\,dx$, $dz = \cos x \,dx \implies z = \sin x$,
then, $$ \int e^x \cos x\,dx = e^x \sin x - \int e^x \sin x \,dx $$ Note the third integral is the original integral, so \begin{align} \int e^x \sin x \,dx & = -e^x \cos x + \int e^x \cos x \,dx \\ & = -e^x\cos x + e^x \sin x - \int e^x\sin x \,dx \\ & = \frac{ e^x \sin x -e^x\cos x }{2} \end{align}

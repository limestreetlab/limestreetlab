Check whether the series $\sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{n!}{n^n}$ converges.
Use the ratio test. \begin{align} \frac{(n+1)!}{(n+1)^{n+1}} / \frac{n!}{n^n} & = \frac{(n+1)!}{(n+1)^{n+1}} \cdot \frac{n^n}{n!} \\ & = \frac{(n+1)n!}{(n+1)^{n+1}} \cdot \frac{n^n}{n!} \\ & = \frac{n^n}{(n+1)^n} \\ & = \left(\frac{n}{n+1}\right)^n \\ & = \left( \frac{1}{1+\frac{1}{n}} \right)^n \\ & = \frac{1}{(1+\frac{1}{n})^n} \\ \end{align} As $n$ becomes large, $$ \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1}{(1+\frac{1}{n})^n} = \frac{1}{e} < 1 $$ Thus, the series $\sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{n!}{n^n}$ converges.

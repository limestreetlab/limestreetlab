Find the radius of convergence for power series $\sum(1+(-2)^n)x^n$.
Recall we can find the radius of convergence $R$ of a power series $\sum a_n x^n$ using either $ L = \lim_{n\to\infty}|\frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n}|$ or $L = \lim_{n\to\infty}\sqrt[n]{|a_n|}$, and let $R = \frac{1}{L}$.

\begin{align} \left|\frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n}\right| & = \left|\frac{1+(-2)^{n+1}}{1+(-2)^n} \right| \\ & = \left|\frac{1+(-2)^n(-2)}{1+(-2)^n} \right| \\ & = \left| \frac{\frac{1}{-2^n} + (-2)}{\frac{1}{-2^n} + 1} \right| \\ & \implies \lim_{n\to\infty}\left| \frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n}\right| = \left| \frac{0 - 2 }{0 + 1} \right| = 2 \end{align} So we have $R = \frac{1}{L} = \frac{1}{2}$.

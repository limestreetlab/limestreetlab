Check whether the series $\sum_{n=1}^\infty ne^{-n^2}$ converges.
Let's use the integral test and let $f(x) = xe^{-x^2}$, which is a decreasing function.

Let $u = -x^2 \implies du = -2x\,dx$, \begin{align} \int_1^\infty xe^{-x^2}\,dx & = -\frac{1}{2}\int_{-1}^{-\infty}e^u\,du \\ & = \left. -\frac{1}{2}e^u \right|_{-1}^{-\infty} \\ & = \frac{1}{2e} \end{align} As the improper integral has a limit, the series converges.

A basketball team, Chicago Labrador, will play a 44-game season.
26 of these games are against Toronto Python and 18 are against Seattle Dolphin.
Assume the Labrador has a winning probability of 0.4 against the Python and 0.7 against the Dolphin.
Game results are independent. What's the probability that
  • Chicago Labrador will win at least 25 games in the season;
  • Chicago Labrador will win more games against Toronto Python than against Seattle Dolphin.
Let $W_T, W_S$ denote winnings against Toronto and Seattle, which are binomial $r.v. $, \begin{align} E(W_T) = (26)(0.4) & = 10.4 \quad & Var(W_T) & = (26)(0.4)(0.6) = 6.24 \\ E(W_S) = (18)(0.7) & = 12.6 \quad & Var(W_S) & = (18)(0.7)(0.3) = 3.78 \end{align} Given independence, \begin{align} & E(W_T + W_S) = E(W_T) + E(W_S) = 23.0 \\ & Var(W_T + W_S) = Var(W_T) + Var(W_S) = 10.02 \end{align} Approximating using normal, \begin{align} P(W_T+W_S \geq 25) & = P(Z \geq \frac{25-23}{\sqrt{10.02}}) \\ & \approx P(Z \geq 0.63) \\ & \approx 1 - P(Z \lt 0.63) \\ & \approx 1 - 0.74 \\ & \approx 0.26 \end{align}
Now, $W_T - W-S$ is also normal with $E(W_T - W_S) = -2.2 \text{ and } Var(W_T - W_S) = 10.02$, \begin{align} P(W_T \gt W_S) & = P(W_T - W_S \geq 1) \\ & = P(Z \geq \frac{3.2}{\sqrt{10.02}}) \\ & \approx P(Z \geq 1.01) = 1 - P(Z \lt 1.01) \\ & \approx 1 - 0.844 \approx 0.16 \end{align} Hence, the probabilities are about $26\% \text{ and } 16\%$, respectively.

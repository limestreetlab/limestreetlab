There are $10$ coins where the $i^{th}$ coin lands head with probability $\frac{i}{10}$.
We pick one of those $10$ coins at random and it shows head. What's the probability that the coin is
  1. The second coin, $i = 2$
  2. The fifth coin, $i = 5$
  3. The tenth coin, $i = 10$
a.
\begin{align} P(i = 2 | H) &= \frac{P(H | i=2)P(i = 2)}{\sum_{j=1}^{10} P(H | i=j)P(i = j)} \\ & = \frac{2/10 \cdot 1/10}{1/10 \cdot 1/10 + 2/10 \cdot 1/10 + \ldots + 9/10 \cdot 1/10 + 10/10 \cdot 1/10} \\ & = \frac{2/10}{1/10 + 2/10 + \ldots + 9/10 + 10/10} \\ &= \frac{2/10}{1/10(1+2+\ldots +9 +10)} \\ &= \frac{2/10}{1/10(55)} \\ & = \frac{2}{10} \cdot \frac{10}{55} \\ & = \frac{2}{55} \approx 0.036 \end{align} b.
\begin{align} P(i = 5 | H) &= \frac{P(H | i=5)P(i = 5)}{\sum_{j=1}^{10} P(H | i=j)P(i = j)} \\ & = \frac{5/10 \cdot 1/10}{1/10 \cdot 1/10 + 2/10 \cdot 1/10 + \ldots + 9/10 \cdot 1/10 + 10/10 \cdot 1/10} \\ & = \frac{5/10}{1/10 + 2/10 + \ldots + 9/10 + 10/10} \\ &= \frac{5/10}{55/10} \\ & = \frac{5}{10} \cdot \frac{10}{55} \\ & = \frac{5}{55} \approx 0.09 \end{align} c.
\begin{align} P(i = 10 | H) &= \frac{P(H | i=10)P(i = 10)}{\sum_{j=1}^{10} P(H | i=j)P(i = j)} \\ & = \frac{10/10 \cdot 1/10}{1/10 \cdot 1/10 + 2/10 \cdot 1/10 + \ldots + 9/10 \cdot 1/10 + 10/10 \cdot 1/10} \\ & = \frac{10/10}{1/10 + 2/10 + \ldots + 9/10 + 10/10} \\ &= \frac{10/10}{55/10} \\ & = \frac{10}{10} \cdot \frac{10}{55} = \frac{10}{55} \approx 0.18 \end{align}

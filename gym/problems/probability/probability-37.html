Inside a box of $15$ balls, $9$ are new and $6$ are used.
$3$ balls are picked at random and used, then returned to the box.
Later, $3$ balls are again picked at random from the box.
What's the probability that all those $3$ balls are new?
There are two random picks of $3$ balls each.
Let $A_i$ denote the first pick where $i$ is the number of used balls picked, so $A_2$ denotes $2$ out of the $3$ balls picked are used.
Let $B$ denote the event that all $3$ balls are new in the second pick, so the desired probability is \begin{align} P(B) &= P(B | A_0)P(A_0) + P(B | A_1)P(A_1) + P(B | A_2)P(A_2) + P(B | A_3)P(A_3) \\ &= \sum_{i=0}^3 \frac{ { {9-3+i} \choose 3} }{ {15 \choose 3} } \cdot \frac{ {6 \choose i} {9 \choose {3-i}} }{ {15 \choose 3} } \\ &\approx 0.09 \end{align}

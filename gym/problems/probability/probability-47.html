When rolling $6$ dice,
  1. What's the probability that all numbers are different?
  2. What's the probability that there are three pairs?
  1. Assume rolling the dice in sequence, the first roll can be any number, the second has to be the remaining $5$ numbers, the third the remaining $4$ numbers, \dots . $$ \frac{6}{6} \cdot \frac{5}{6} \cdot \frac{4}{6} \cdot \frac{3}{6} \cdot \frac{2}{6} \cdot \frac{1}{6} = \frac{720}{46656} \approx 1.5\% $$ Alternatively, the number of possible ordered outcomes for $6$ dice is $6^6$ and number of outcomes of each number appearing once is $6!$, so $$ \frac{6!}{6^6} = \frac{720}{46656} \approx 1.5\% $$
  2. The $3$ different numbers can be picked in $\binom{6}{3}$ ways. After the $3$ numbers are picked, each of them has to fill $2$ slots in the $6$ slots. There are \binom{6}{2} ways for the first number, \binom{4}{2} ways for the second number, and \binom{2}{2} for the third number. The probability of three pairs is therefore $$ \frac{\binom{6}{3} \cdot \binom{6}{2} \cdot \binom{4}{2} \cdot \binom{2}{2} }{6^6} = \frac{1800}{46656} \approx 3.9\% $$

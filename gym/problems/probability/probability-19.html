For $f_{X,Y}(x,y) = \frac{e^{-x/y}e^{-y}}{y} \qquad 0\lt x, y\lt \infty$
Find $P(X \gt 1 | Y=y)$.
First, compute the conditional density, \begin{align} f_{X|Y}(x|y) &= \frac{f_{X,Y}(x,y)}{f_Y(y)} \\ & = \frac{f_{X,Y}(x,y)}{\int_{-\infty}^\infty f_{X,Y}(x,y) dx} \\ & = \frac{ e^{-x/y}e^{-y}/y } { \frac{e^{-y}}{y}\int_0^\infty e^{-x/y} dx } \\ & = \frac{ e^{-x/y}e^{-y}/y } {\frac{e^{-y}}{y} \left((-ye^{-x/y})\bigg|_0^\infty \right) }\\ & = \frac{ e^{-x/y}e^{-y}/y } {e^{-y}} \\ & = \frac{1}{y} e^{-x/y} \\ \end{align} Hence, \begin{align} P(X\gt 1 | Y=y) &= \int_1^\infty \frac{1}{y} e^{-x/y} dx \\ & = (-e^{-x/y})\bigg |_1^\infty \\ & = (0) - (-e^{-1/y}) = e^{-1/y} \\ \end{align}

Consider the following game.
Tiger rolls a fair die. If he rolls 3 or less, $\{1,2,3\}$, he loses the game immediately.
Otherwise, he is dealt as many cards from a 52-card deck as the number on the die—that is, $\{4,5,6\}$.
Tiger wins only if all four aces are among the dealt cards.
Compute the probability of winning this game.
Let $W$ be the event of winning and $R_i$ of rolling $i$ on the die.
The game is lost immediately if the roll is $\{1,2,3\}$, so $P(W|R_1) = P(W|R_2) = P(W|R_3) = 0$.
Moreover, \begin{align} P(W|R_4) & = \frac{4 \choose 4}{52 \choose 4} \\ P(W|R_5) & = \frac{ {4 \choose 4}{48 \choose 1} }{52 \choose 5} \\ P(W|R_6) & = \frac{ {4 \choose 4}{48 \choose 2 } }{52 \choose 6} \\ \end{align} Hence, \begin{align} P(W) &= \frac{1}{6}\cdot\frac{4 \choose 4}{52 \choose 4} + \frac{1}{6}\cdot\frac{ {4 \choose 4}{48 \choose 1} }{52 \choose 5} + \frac{1}{6}\cdot\frac{ {4 \choose 4}{48 \choose 2 } }{52 \choose 6} \\ &\approx 0.0013\% \end{align}

In a multiple-choice test, each question has $ m $ choices.
Peter has probability $ p $ of knowing the answer. When Peter does not know the answer, he guesses randomly.
  1. What's the conditional probability that Peter really knew the answer to a question given a correct answer?
  2. What if we let $ m $ and $ p $ be $ 5 $ and $ 0.5 $?
Let $ K $ and $ C $ denote the events that Peter really knows the answer and that a question is answered correctly.
\begin{align} P(K | C) & = \frac{ P(K \cap C) }{ P(C) } \\ & = \frac{ P(K) P(C | K) }{ P(K) P(C | K) + P(K^c) P(C | K^c) } \\ & = \frac{ p \cdot 1 }{ p \cdot 1 + (1-p) \frac{1}{m} } \\ & = \frac{m \cdot p}{m \cdot p + (1-p) } \\ & = \frac{m \cdot p}{1 + p(m - 1)} \end{align} Letting $ p=0.5 $ and $ m = 5 $, we get $ P(K | C) = \frac{2.5}{3} = \frac{5}{6} \approx 0.83 $.

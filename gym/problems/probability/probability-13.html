$X$ and $Y$ have joint density $f_{X,Y}(x,y) = 2e^{-x}e^{-2y} \qquad 0\lt x,y \lt \infty$.
Find
  1. $P(X \gt 1, Y \lt 1)$
  2. $P(X \lt Y)$
  3. $P(X \lt k) $
a. \begin{align} P(X \gt 1, Y \lt 1) & = \int_0^1 \int_1^\infty 2e^{-x}e^{-2y} dx dy \\ & = \int_0^1 2e^{-2y} e^{-1} dy \\ & = 2e^{-1} \left( e^{-2y}\frac{-1}{2}\right) \Bigg|_0^1 \\ & = 2e^{-1} \left( \frac{-e^{-2}}{2} + \frac{1}{2} \right) \\ & = e^{-1}(1 -e^{-2} ) \end{align}
b. \begin{align} P(X \lt Y) & = \iint\limits_{(x,y):x\lt y}2e^{-x}e^{-2y}dxdy \\ & = \int_0^\infty \int_0^y 2e^{-x}e^{-2y}dxdy \\ & = \int_0^\infty 2e^{-2y} (-e^{-x})\bigg|_0^y dy \\ & = \int_0^\infty 2e^{-2y} (1 - e^{-y})dy \\ & = \int_0^\infty 2e^{-2y}dy - \int_0^\infty 2e^{-3y} dy \\ & = -e^{2y} \Big|_0^\infty + \frac{2}{3}e^{-3y} \Big|_0^\infty \\ & = (0) - (-1) + (0) - (\frac{2}{3}) = \frac{1}{3} \end{align}
c. \begin{align} P(X \lt k) &= \int_0^k f_X(x) dx \\ &= \int_0^k \int_{-\infty}^\infty f_{X,Y}(x,y) dydx \\ & = \int_0^k \int_{0}^\infty 2e^{-x}e^{-2y} dydx \\ & = \int_0^k 2e^{-x}e^{-2y}\frac{-1}{2} \bigg|_0^\infty dx \\ & = \int_0^k (0) - (-e^{-x}) dx \\ & = -e^{-x} \bigg |_0^k \\ & = (-e^{-k}) - (-e^{0}) = 1 - e^{-k} \end{align}

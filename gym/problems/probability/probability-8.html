A 1-person toilet at Stardust Coffee has an average occupancy time of 10 minutes.
You want to pee badly but someone goes in right before you.
If the occupancy time is exponentially distributed, what's the probability you have to wait
  • more than 10 minutes;
  • between 10 and 20 minutes;
  • more than 20 minutes, which will explode.
Recall for exponential distribution, $$ f_X(x) = \lambda e^{-\lambda x} \quad \text{for } x\geq 0 , \qquad F_X(x) = 1-e^{-\lambda x} \quad \text{for } x\geq 0$$
Simply plugging the rate $\lambda = \frac{1}{E(X)} = \frac{1}{10} \text{ into } F_X(x)$, \begin{align} P(T \gt 10) & = 1 - P(T \leq 10) \\ & = 1 - (1 - e^{-\frac{10}{10}}) \\ & = e^{-1} \approx 0.37 \end{align} \begin{align} P(10 \lt T \lt 20) & = F(20) - F(10) \\ & = (1 - e^{-\frac{20}{10}}) - (1 - e^{-\frac{10}{10}}) \\ & = -e^{-2} + e^{-1} \\ & \approx -0.14 + 0.37 = 0.23 \end{align} $$ P(T \gt 20) \approx 0.37 - 0.23 = 0.14. $$
